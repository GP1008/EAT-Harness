Phase 01
Days 1 – 30
Map the operational waste
Sit with ops and escrow teams. Find exactly where time is lost to manual data entry before building anything.
Establish an AI Registry
Governance
Create a registry of every AI tool currently in use, by whom, and for what purpose. Establish visibility before establishing rules.
Stand Up Governance Committee
Governance
Cross-functional: legal/compliance, ops, IT, leadership. Reference NAIC principles — Oversight, Operations, Compliance, Culture. Lightweight, not bureaucratic.
Process Audit — Baseline Timing
Operations
Payoff management, HOA estoppel tracking, and closing condition clearance. Time each step. Find the re-keying. Find the wait. Build the baseline.
Compliance Gap Analysis
Regulatory
Map emerging AI requirements against current operations. Flag TRAIGA (TX 2026) and AZ HB 2394 disclosure/impersonation rules. Identify the gaps before they become fines.
Phase 02
Days 31 – 60
Launch a targeted pilot
Pick one high-friction area. Deploy a 60-day pilot. Measure time saved and error reduction. Show the team AI assists — it does not replace.
Deploy Payoff Expiry Automation
Operations
Build countdown clocks. Alert when payoff nears expiry. Integrate with case notebook. Measure time saved on payoff re-ordering.
Establish Audit Trail for Document Intake
Regulatory
Hash-chain every document upload. Log identity verification events. Build TRAIGA readiness into the intake process, not as retrofit.
HITL Review Protocol
Human-in-Loop
Define when AI surfaces exceptions and when humans override. Document every override. Build trust through transparency.
Measure & Report (30-60 Day Mark)
Measurement
Hours saved. Error reduction. Compliance events caught. Cost of pilot vs. value unlocked. Use this to build case for phase 3.
Phase 03
Days 61 – 90
Measure, layer, and expand
Use early wins to build momentum. Show measurable ROI. Add governance controls. Propose the next pilot.
Governance Hardening
Governance
Layer 2 admission controls. Policy guidance layer. Expand the audit trail to all AI-assisted decisions. Make it audit-defensible.
HOA Estoppel Automation Design
Operations
Design (but do not yet deploy) AI-assisted estoppel tracking. Map to title commitment conditions. Build on lessons from payoff automation.
Compliance Report & Leadership Review
Regulatory
Formal report to leadership: TRAIGA readiness, AZ HB 2394 safe harbor status, governance committee findings. Road to multi-year scale.
Phase 4 Planning & Stakeholder Alignment
Human-in-Loop
Propose next pilots. Lock in budget. Set 12-month roadmap. Position EAT Harness as the agency's AI operating system, not a one-off tool.
Four strategic pillars
⚖️
AI Governance Framework
Governance is the product. Not a checklist — a living system that makes AI accountable before it makes decisions, not after.
AI Registry — visibility of all tools and uses
Governance Committee — cross-functional oversight
Audit Trail — every decision logged and traceable
🏛️
Regulatory Alignment
Compliance-by-design means the system is built around the rules — not patched to meet them after the fact.
TRAIGA (TX 2026) — governance documentation ready
AZ HB 2394 — impersonation & disclosure safe harbor
TRID/RESPA — wire instruction and timing compliance
⚡
Operational Efficiency
Scale transaction volume without scaling headcount. AI handles the drudgery. Humans handle the judgment.
Payoff automation — expiry countdown clocks
Condition tracking — commitment state machine
Exception alerts — surface blockers early, always
👤
Human-in-the-Loop Always
AI assists professional judgment. It does not substitute for it. Especially on commitments, closings, and fund releases.
HITL Review Protocol — when AI surfaces, humans decide
Override logging — every deviation is audited
Professional accountability — licensed escrow officer always in chain
Questions that show strategic thinking
Ops Intelligence
What are the biggest operational pain points today — manual processes, compliance bottlenecks, closing delays?
Shows you need real data before building anything. Signals practitioner, not theorist.
Leadership Alignment
How does leadership currently view AI adoption — where's the appetite for risk vs. the urgency for reward?
Surfaces political landscape. Tells you whether to lead with efficiency or compliance framing.
Compliance Gap
What existing compliance infrastructure is in place for title and escrow operations — and where are the gaps for AI specifically?
Positions you as building on what exists. Shows you won't create duplication or conflict.
Tech Stack
What's the current tech stack and vendor landscape? Any plans for AZ or TX market expansion?
Integration reality check. Expansion signals where your governance architecture becomes more valuable.
Success Definition
What does success look like for this role at 90 days — and at 12 months?
Closes the loop on the roadmap. Shows you think in measurable outcomes, not activities.
Team Readiness
How is the team currently thinking about AI — early adopters, skeptics, both? What's shaped that?
Culture intelligence. Change management is 80% of implementation. This tells you what you're walking into.
Regulatory framework — what governs this work
NAIC AI Principles
Oversight, Operations, Compliance, and Culture. The national framework for responsible AI use in insurance and title. Governance committee structure maps directly to these four pillars.
NAIC Principles on AI · 2020 (updated)
Texas TRAIGA 2026
Requires "governance by design" documentation for AI used in consequential decisions. EAT Harness audit trail and AI registry satisfies this requirement structurally — not as a retrofit.
TX SB 2068 · Effective 2026
Arizona HB 2394
Anti-impersonation and deepfake disclosure requirements. Governed document intake with identity verification and hash-chained audit trail creates safe harbor against seller impersonation claims.
AZ HB 2394 · Active
TRID / RESPA Overlap
Closing disclosure timing rules, wire instruction liability, and escrow acknowledgment requirements all create documentation obligations. Governance layer satisfies these as a byproduct of operation.
12 CFR 1026 / 12 CFR 1024
